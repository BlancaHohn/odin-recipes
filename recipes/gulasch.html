Most amazing Gulasch


Gulasch?


Gulasch has its origin in Hungary and made its way to Austrian's culinary favorites in the 19th century.

Ingredients


  • 500 g beef (from the shoulder)
  • 500 grams of onion
  • 3 tablespoons butter or lard
  • 3 pcs garlic cloves
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 tsp paprika (spicy)
  • 1 pinch ground pepper
  • 2 tbsp paprika (noble sweet)
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 spray vinegar
  • 1 pinch of cumin
  • 1 tsp marjoram (finely chopped)
  • 0.75 l beef soup

The Steps


  1. For the Gulasch, cut the meat into approx. 2 cm cubes. Finely chop the onion and sauté in a saucepan with butter or lard until golden.
  2. Now season the sliced ​​meat with salt and pepper and fry with the onion until it has turned gray all over.
  3. Then add 1 tablespoon of tomato paste and also roast well so that it loses the acidity.
  4. Then add the finely chopped garlic cloves and the hot and sweet paprika and roast briefly (not too long, otherwise it will be bitter).
  5. Deglaze with a dash of vinegar (or red wine), pour in the soup and season with caraway (generously) and marjoram.
  6. Cover the Gulasch and let it stew for about 1 - 1.5 hours. Finally bind with flour if necessary.
  7. Enjoy!
